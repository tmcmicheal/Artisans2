About Artisans

Artisans is a non-profit agency providing services necessary for individual job development, employment support and employment opportunities in the community for persons with disabilities.


Overview

Artisans strives to provide each of our clients the services necessary to ensure employment goals can be accomplished and provide stable and supportive work environments. Artisans provides employment and community access services for persons with intellectual and physical disabilities.

Each client Artisans serves is a unique individual with specific skills, abilities and talents. Because of this, our agency prides itself in offering services and employment opportunities as diversified as those that make up our organization.

Artisans' ability to transform to meet the needs of our clients, innovative job developing techniques and dedicated board members and staff are key components for our continued success. Over the years, Artisans has grown, established many strong employer and community relationships through all of its programs and is the largest Group Supported Employment provider in Spokane County.



History

Artisans has been a proud partner of the Spokane Community since 1987. Our services continually transform to meet the needs of our clients. Artisans began as an agency whose employment offerings centered around on-site woodwork. Over the past 25 years, however, Artisans has grown and undergone many changes.


In 2004 Artisans began to take a new direction that emphasized community-based employment. Now our clients work in community-based jobs providing various services for private and commercial employers. Artisans began providing employment services for less than 10 adults with disabilities, and today provides employment support for more than 100 individuals. Artisans provides job developing and coaching to persons with disabilities for both Individual and Group Supported Employment, and Community Access services.


Programs

Individual Supported Employment (ISE)

Our agency offers a variety of services and support throughout the entire employment process. Our staff works diligently to offer job placements in the community and provides personalized support for each individual, centering efforts on the individual's interests and abilities.

Group Supported Employment (GSE)

Our Group Supported Employment services offer job positions in the community with continuous support provided by staff members. Individuals work in small groups and earn pay based on productivity. Staff members provide training, job development and the supervision necessary to create a constructive work environment.

Community Access

Participants enrolled in this program will receive services focused on activities and goals related to community engagement and integration.

Our Partnerships
Education is key to the success of our employment programs. Many members of the community have misconceptions and fears about individuals with intellectual disabilities. Through our efforts to develop employment opportunities in the community for our clients, we strive first and foremost to help employers understand hiring a person with a disability should not be seen as a charitable obligation. A person should be hired, whether he or she has a disability, based on grounds that he or she can perform the required job duties for that position.

Artisans is set apart from its competitors as it is the largest Group Supported Employment provider in Spokane County. Artisans has taken creative avenues and approaches to develop group job sites in the community. One approach has been the development of several work contracts with local businesses, such as Waste Management and Pepsi Co., 2nd Harvest Food Bank, Earthworks Recycling, Saranac Building and Community Building. The other is the development of mobile work crews that are managed in house which offer landscaping, janitorial, vending and recycling services to commercial businesses as well as residential customers.

One of our work crews has been employed by the Pepsi Bottling Co. for more than six years assisting with sorting and processing of damaged products. Mike Duquette, Product Availability Manager, states that having the Artisans work crew at the Pepsi "…has added value to our lives. I can tell you that because they want to do a good job, they like what they do and we enjoy them being here."
The work they do saves the company on average more than 10,000 cases of product each year.



Employer Testimonials


Through our Individual Employment program we support our clients in finding competitive employment positions in the community. We offer training support to a client as long as needed, which helps alleviate the responsibility of the hiring company to offer the extra training hours as well as provide ongoing support after initial training is completed. Our staff will go through the training process with the client hired in the position to ensure any extra training and coaching our staff provides will be in line with the company's standards and expectations. Some of the businesses who have hired our clients are Trader Joe's, Walmart, Safeway, Timber Creek Grill Buffett, Office Depot and 14th and Grand Salon.
